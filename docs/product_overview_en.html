Product Overview · 2026

From spec to production — on your own infrastructure.

DevTeam Hub runs your entire agile delivery process — tickets, sprints, automated ceremonies and automatic testing — and wraps it in a local AI engine that never sends a byte to the cloud. One self-hosted platform, from the product manager's first story to a monitored production server.

📋 Why agile, ticket management & automatic testing

Software teams fail in the gaps — between a fuzzy idea and a clear story, between an estimate and reality, between "done" and "actually tested." DevTeam Hub closes those gaps with three disciplines, in one place:

🏃

An agile process

Work flows in short, predictable iterations (sprints) with a single current sprint, clear goals and a visible burn-down — so priorities are explicit and progress is measurable.

🎫

Ticket management

Every unit of work is a ticket with a lifecycle (draft → approved → estimated → in delivery → done), an owner, an assignee and a full audit trail.

🧪

Automatic testing

CI builds, parsed test results, and AI test review keep quality continuous — a ticket isn't "done" until its tests are green.

The result: nothing falls through the cracks. Specs are written well, estimated honestly, delivered in iterations, tested automatically, shipped to a monitored server — and every step is tracked.

🤖 One platform, powered by a local AI engine

DevTeam Hub embeds a large-language model running on your own on-prem hardware (Ollama on a Mac mini), reached over a plain REST API on the LAN. No code, ticket text, or customer data ever leaves your network — exactly what regulated, NDA-bound and air-gapped teams need.

✍️

Better specs

AI readiness checks verify story-telling and bounce thin tickets back to the owner.

🧩

Smarter estimates

AI breaks stories into tasks and calibrates estimates against your historical estimate-vs-actual data.

🔍

Continuous review

AI reviews diffs (Go/Ruby/C#/Node) and Cucumber tests; "fix that bug" proposes a concrete fix.

📄

Docs & presentations

Specs, risk-management docs and status presentations generated from live project data.

📈

Live analysis

Real-time sprint health, estimation accuracy and per-developer performance.

💬

Ask anything

A project-scoped chat that knows your repo, tickets, sprint and team (see below).

🏃 Iterations & sprints

Work is delivered in time-boxed sprints that belong to a project.

⭐

One current sprint

Each project has exactly one active sprint. Setting a new one current automatically closes the previous — there's never ambiguity about "what are we working on now."

📊

Estimated vs. actual

Every project lists its sprints with total estimated and total actual hours, plus a task-based progress bar.

🧩

Tasks drive progress

Stories break into small, estimable tasks; completion rolls up into a 🧩 % and ⏱ done/total h badge shown wherever the ticket appears.

👥

The sprint team

A sprint's members are derived from the people on its tickets, so the team is always accurate.

📅 Automated agile ceremonies — built for remote teams

Every ceremony has a dedicated page with the team's video call on top (Jitsi) and the right data below — so a distributed team runs the full agile cadence without leaving the tool or stitching together separate apps.

🗣️

Daily Standup

Per-developer "what I did yesterday / what I'll do today", straight from their tasks and tickets.

📋

Sprint Planning

Team capacity, what still needs estimation, and a backlog you can pull from — live.

🔍

Backlog Refinement

Surfaces tickets missing estimates or detail, so the team refines them together.

🎬

Sprint Review / Demo

Exactly what shipped this sprint, ready to demo, plus carry-over.

🔁

Retrospective

Sprint performance vs. previous sprints + a per-developer scorecard (story quality, estimation, tests).

🎥

Jitsi video backend

Each ceremony embeds a self-hosted (or public) Jitsi room — no Zoom, no per-seat licensing, your video stays on your infrastructure.

Remote-first: stand-ups, planning, reviews and retros all run with the camera bar docked at the top of the page. A toolbar contact finder lets anyone video-call or message a teammate from any screen.

🧪 Automatic testing, end to end

⚙️

CI & test results

Jenkins/Gitea builds feed parsed pass/fail results onto the ticket and the CI dashboard.

🔍

AI code & test review

The AI reviews diffs and Cucumber feature files, flagging bugs and missing scenarios.

📝

In-app test editor

Edit a ticket's .feature files in a dark, Gherkin-highlighted editor and review them with the AI — right from the ticket's Files-Changed panel.

💬 Ask your project anything

Open Chat with AI from any project. It loads that project's git repo, tickets, sprint, messages and a per-developer performance summary — so the answers are grounded in your real data.
